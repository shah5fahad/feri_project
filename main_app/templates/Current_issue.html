Global Trade, Climate Change, and the Spread of Invasive Alien Species: Impacts on Ecosystem Services

This paper explores how global trade and climate change together accelerate the spread of Invasive Alien Species (IAS), threatening biodiversity and ecosystem services worldwide. It highlights how trade routes and climatic shifts aid IAS survival, impacting agriculture, forestry, water systems, and human well-being, while stressing the urgent need for integrated ecological, economic, and policy measures.

Published: 24 Oct 2025 | Category: Environmental Science

Biodiversity Conservation and Phytodiversity Assessment in the Catchment Area of Runjh Dam, Panna (M.P.)

This study evaluates the ecological impact of the Runjh Dam catchment on plant diversity using quadrat and transect-based analysis supported by GIS mapping. Findings highlight major shifts in species distribution, soil changes, and habitat fragmentation, while also stressing the role of afforestation, buffer zones, and community-based conservation in sustainable development.

Published: 24 Oct 2025 | Category: Environmental Science

Macroinvertebrates as Indicators of Trophic Status & Water Quality of Kaliasote Dam

This ecological study investigates macroinvertebrate diversity in Kaliasote Dam, highlighting their role in determining water quality and trophic status. The findings reveal moderate pollution, eutrophic tendencies, and the presence of tolerant species, underscoring the need for regular ecological monitoring and sustainable management.

Published: 24 Oct 2025 | Category: Environmental Science

First Record of Leucistic Indian Pied Starling in Keonjhar, Odisha

This study documents the first observation of a leucistic Indian Pied Starling (Gracupica contra) in Keonjhar District, Odisha, India. The bird exhibited large white feathers with preserved black soft-part pigmentation (normal eye and bill), consistent with leucism rather than albinism. This represents one of the few reports of leucism in this species from eastern India.

Published: 6 August 2025 | Category: Ornithology & Ecology

GIS-Based Analysis of Urban Sprawl in Narsinghgarh, MP

This GIS-based study analyzes urban sprawl and land use transformation in Narsinghgarh, Madhya Pradesh from 2005 to 2015. The research reveals significant expansion of built-up areas (15% of total land), consumption of agricultural land (52%), and patterns of development along transportation corridors. Population projections indicate continued growth, necessitating sustainable urban planning strategies.

Published: 6 August 2025 | Category: Urban Planning & GIS
